Joe Skalski is a firefighter for two communities - the town of Arlington Heights, Illinois and the village of Cary, Illinois. Though he was half a country away from the attacks, he, like all police and firefighters, felt its effects very closely.  When he saw the famous photo of three firefighters covered in ash raising the U.S. flag over the rubble, he surely felt the same pangs of pride and solidarity through tragedy that many of us felt.  Following the tragedy, he traveled with others from his department to serve in the honor guard at funerals and wakes for his New York brothers.  That experience and the photo's lasting image sparked a very unique idea in his mind.

Joe loves to cross stitch, a hobby that has been the root of much ribbing and joking in the firehouse from his fellow firefighters.  It is a hobby he learned while he was confined to bed rest for four months following a car accident.  The others in the firestation call him "Grandma" when he's stitching at the house. 

But when he started on the project of stitching the famous photo by Thomas E. Franklin, the others held their tongues.  He worked with Thomas and his lawyers to obtain permission to use the protected photo that had won the Pulitzer Prize.  He then transferred the photo to a counted cross stitch grid and began the work of stitching the 66,000 stitches.

At 14 squares per inch and with most squares being a different color to capture the depth of the photo, from a distance it's impossible to tell the difference between his finished piece and the photo.  It's not until you are within a foot or two that you can tell it is made up of tiny stitched squares.  The project took Joe a full year to complete, but it's his way of remembering those brothers lost on September 11.

Joe is looking to sell his finished work to raise money for the widows and orphans of the fallen New York firefighters.  He's hoping to raise as much as $10,000 for the fund.  Joe has used his talent to remember his heroes while becoming a hero himself for his patience, time, effort, and generosity.
