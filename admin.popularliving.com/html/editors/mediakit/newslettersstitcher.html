 BJ & Reader's Tip Corner:


Tip #1:
Whenever you have a large cross-stitch project: begin stitching in the areas with intense solid stitching.  When stitching in such solid areas, be certain to enter your needle from the backside of the linen perfectly straight, and not on an angle allowing your needle to split the fibers that may occupy that hole.

Tip #2:
When laundering a 100% linen project, carefully hand wash with Woolite and follow these simple suggestions to eliminate wrinkles.  First, attempt to dry-press with a hot iron to eliminate all wrinkles.  If this is not successful, very lightly mist from the backside of linen, and immediately steam-press with a warm iron, placing the linen between two layers of either cheesecloth or muslin.  Press only from the backside, until it is wrinkle-free.  If your iron is hot enough, you should be able to rid the wrinkles.   

Tip #3:
The backstitch highlights any of the detail of the various motifs that are within your cross-stitch project.  To perfect the backstitch, it must be executed with a loose tension, even more so than the cross-stitches.  Remember, if you pull the backstitches too tight, all of your loose tension on your cross-stitches would be in vain.  They would simply fall between the stitches.  Not only should the backstitch be the last thing you complete, but it also needs the most of your focus on stitch tension.  Keep in mind, loose is better than tight.

 Reader Questions & Answers:


Q: I accidentally scorched my project when I was ironing it.  How can I get this out?  Would it be easier to tea dye?
A: Tea dying sounds more like it would work on this. Scorching is extremely hard to get out. You might try soaking the area in lemon juice --not allowing it to be against a stitched area on the front side - and placing in a sunny area. Then rinse thoroughly.

Q: What does ‘over two threads’ mean within the cross stitch instructions?
A: ‘While cross-stitching, when it says ‘over two threads’ it actually means stitching over 4 squares instead of one square.  (The correct terminology though is "threads" not squares).
