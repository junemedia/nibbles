DIRECTIONS:
To start:
1.  Stain the frame with Minwax Early American.
2.  Seal and sand the insert and then base with Lamp Black.
3.  Apply the basic design and base as follows:
     Old Coffee Tin: Sapphire + Blue Violet (1/2-1/2)
     Lid: Neutral Gray Toning
     Book: Country Red
     Butter Crock: Grey Sky
     Coffee Pot: Ice Blue Mix--Ice Blue + Light Buttermilk (1/2-1/2)
     Rim:  Country Red
     Crockery Creamer:  Western Sunset
4.  Transfer the interior detail.

Old Coffee Tin:
Lettering is Light Buttermilk.  When dry, shade Deep Midnight Blue and then Deep Midnight Blue and a touch of Lamp Black.  The lid is shaded with a soft float of Lamp Black.  Highlights on the lid and coffee can are reverse floats and pitty-pats of Light Buttermilk.

Butter Crock:
1.  Detailing is Nightfall and the pollen in the center of the flower is Western Sunset.
2.  Shading is Neutral Gray Toning, then Nightfall.
3.  Add a final shading of Neutral Gray Toning & a touch of Lamp Black.
4.  Add accents of color with Nightfall and Country Red.  Highlights are Light Buttermilk.

Coffee Pot:
1.  Shading is Neutral Gray Toning and then Neutral Gray Toning with a touch of Lamp Black.
2.  Accent with Country Red.  Shade the rim and handle with Candy Bar and a touch of Lamp Black      & highlight the handles, lid, rim,      spout, and the body of the coffee pot with Light Buttermilk.
3.  The small areas are reverse floats and the larger areas are pitty-pats.

Crockery Creamer:
1.  Wash in stripes with Nightfall.
2.  Shade the stripes with Deep Midnight Blue, then shade the rest of the creamer with Burnt Sienna.
3.  The second shading color over the Burnt Sienna is Burnt Umber.
4.  When dry, highlight with Light Buttermilk.

 Finish:
1.  Float a shelf under all the objects; first with Burnt Umber and then with Burnt Sienna and  Western Sunset (3/4–1/4).
2.  The reflected light on the left side of all the objects is a soft float of French Gray Blue.
3.  Varnish with 3 to 4 coats and then buff softly with #0000 steel wool and glue into place.
